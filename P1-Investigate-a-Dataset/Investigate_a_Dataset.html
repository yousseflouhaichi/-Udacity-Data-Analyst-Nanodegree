Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset is taken from Kaggle and it collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.

  • PatientId: Identification of a patient
  • AppointmentID: Identification of each appointment
  • Gender: Male or Female . gender of the patient
  • ScheduledDay: The day someone called or registered the appointment, this is before appointment of course.
  • AppointmentDay: The day of the actuall appointment, when they have to visit the doctor.
  • Age: The age of the patient.
  • Neighbourhood: Appointment location.
  • Scholarship: Indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program
  • Hipertension: Indicates whether or not the patient have hipertension
  • Diabetes: Indicates whether or not the patient have diabetes
  • Alcoholism Indicates whether or not the patient have alcoholism
  • Handcap Indicates whether or not the patient has a handicap
  • SMS_received Indicates whether or not at least 1 message was sent to the patient
  • No-show Indicates whether or not the patient showed up to their appointment

Question(s) for Analysis¶

Which gender has the highest average age?

What are the characteristics of patients who do not appear on their scheduled appointment date?

What are the neighborhoods with the lowest attendance rate?

def compute_stats(df, col, query):
    
    """
    Generates statistics about a given DataFrame column under a condition.

    This function perform and return some statistics over a DataFrame column including
    absolute value counts and value counts under a given condition
        
    Arguments:
        data : DataFrame
        col : string
            Column name from df
        query : string
            The query string to apply on df
             
    Returns:
        result_df : DataFrame
            
    """
    #Creating dataframe that contains absolute value counts
    result_df = df[col].value_counts().to_frame(name='Total')
    
    #Applying query and storing results
    filtred_df = df.query(query)
    
    #Adding partial value counts to the dataframe
    result_df['Filtred_total'] = filtred_df[col].value_counts()
    
    #Adding the proportion of filtred results
    result_df['Proportion'] = result_df['Filtred_total']/result_df['Total']
    
    
    return(result_df)

Conclusions¶

Following this study, Here are some of the results we got:

1-Most of the patients are females.
2-Females average age is higher that males.
3-The attendance rate of those who received at least one SMS is higher than those who didn't
4-Patients who are not enrolled in a scholarship have higher attendance rate than those who do.
5-Attendance rate is high in the mornings and early evenings and lower in late evenings and afternoons.
6-We were able to see the attendance and the ordred attendance rate per neighborhood.

LImitation:
From the date columns we can see that this observations were spread out only across a month, working on a snapshot of data prevent us from providing a complete picture of the data. if data were spread out over a longer periode we could have discovered some patterns over months, semesters or maybe years.
